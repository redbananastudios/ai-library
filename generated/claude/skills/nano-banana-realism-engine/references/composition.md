# Composition

Composition should make the image feel captured by a person, not assembled by software.

## Default framing rules

- Slightly off-center framing is preferred.
- Human eye-level perspective is the default.
- Cropped edges are acceptable and often beneficial.
- Leave some dead space only when the platform or ad layout needs it.
- Match the crop to the platform:
  - 1:1 or 4:5 for most Facebook/Meta feed work
  - 9:16 for stories/reels if requested
  - landscape for headers or website hero uses

## Scene-specific composition

### People
- Favor candid half-body or environmental portrait framing
- Let hands interact with the environment or product naturally
- Keep perspective believable for the chosen camera

### Products
- Hero the product through use, not just isolation
- Maintain realistic scale against surfaces and hands
- Use depth and foreground/background elements to avoid catalog flatness

### Vehicles and taxis
- Avoid impossible road perspectives
- Keep wheels, reflections, and body lines believable
- Show use context: curbside, street, pickup, rain, commute, airport, night rank, etc.

### Moving and removals
- Show effort, movement, boxes, doorways, vans, hallways, driveways, loading steps
- Preserve realism in body posture and weight distribution
- Use documentary framing over theatrical action shots

## Cropping guidance

Allow:
- partially cropped shoulders
- partially cropped furniture
- foreground obstruction
- slight tilt if smartphone candid

Avoid:
- accidental amputations at joints
- symmetry unless clearly intentional
- empty sterile margins
