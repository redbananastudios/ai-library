# Camera Movement Presets - Complete Reference (70+)

## Standard Camera Movements (18)

### Dolly Movements
- **Dolly In** — camera moves forward toward subject, increasing scale
- **Dolly Out** — camera moves backward away from subject, decreasing scale
- **Dolly Left** — camera trucks left (horizontal movement to the left)
- **Dolly Right** — camera trucks right (horizontal movement to the right)

### Pan & Tilt
- **Pan Left** — camera rotates horizontally left (yaw axis)
- **Pan Right** — camera rotates horizontally right (yaw axis)
- **Tilt Up** — camera tilts upward (pitch axis, looking up)
- **Tilt Down** — camera tilts downward (pitch axis, looking down)

### Vertical & Rotation
- **Crane Up** — camera moves upward smoothly (vertical lift)
- **Crane Down** — camera moves downward smoothly (vertical descent)
- **360 Orbit** — camera circles around subject at constant distance

### Lens-Based
- **Zoom In** — focal length decreases, subject gets larger
- **Zoom Out** — focal length increases, subject gets smaller

### Quality Presets
- **Handheld** — slight wobble, documentary-style motion, human feel
- **Steadicam** — smooth, gliding motion with subtle weight shifts
- **Static** — no camera movement, locked tripod

## Advanced Movements (40+)

### Fast/Dynamic
- **Whip Pan** — fast pan with motion blur, transitions between subjects
- **Crash Zoom In** — snap zoom, sudden focal length change, high-impact
- **YoYo Zoom** — zoom in then out, or out then in, bouncy rhythm
- **Bullet Time** — slow-motion with camera rotation, dramatic effect
- **Fast Cut** — quick transition between compositions

### Through-Object / Spatial
- **Through Object In** — camera pushes through foreground element (window, curtain, branches)
- **Through Object Out** — camera pulls back through foreground obstacle
- **Around Object** — camera circles behind or around physical barrier
- **Super Dolly Out** — extreme pullback, revealing scale and context
- **Dutch Angle** — camera tilts horizontally (roll axis), creating psychological unease

### Rotational (Roll, Pitch, Yaw)
- **3D Rotation** — combination of roll (spin), pitch (tilt), yaw (pan)
- **Spiral** — combined orbit + tilt creating spiral motion
- **Rotation In** — camera rotates while moving forward
- **Rotation Out** — camera rotates while moving backward

### Specialized Movements
- **Arc Right** — curved camera path moving right in an arc
- **Arc Left** — curved camera path moving left in an arc
- **Figure-8** — camera traces figure-eight pattern around subject
- **Lateral Slide** — sideways tracking without pan or tilt
- **Pendulum** — back-and-forth swinging motion, rhythmic
- **Wiggle** — slight vibration, handheld shake without major movement
- **Lift & Pan** — crane up while panning, two-axis combined

## Timelapse / Special Effects (12+)

### Timelapse Presets
- **Glam Timelapse** — stylized fast-motion, beauty/luxury contexts, elegant speed
- **Human Timelapse** — natural-speed fast-motion, daily routines, personal scale
- **Landscape Timelapse** — wide-view fast-motion, environment, sky/weather changes
- **Practical Timelapse** — action-based fast-motion (construction, assembly, cooking)

### Effects
- **Film Grain** — adds 16mm or 35mm film stock grain texture
- **Anamorphic Flare** — lens flare characteristic of anamorphic cinema lenses
- **Bokeh Shift** — changing depth of field, focus breathing
- **Light Bloom** — bright areas glow and bloom, ethereal feel
- **Chromatic Aberration** — subtle color separation, lens artifact aesthetic

| Tone | Feel | Use Case |
|------|------|----------|
| **Smooth** | Gliding, fluid, professional | Brand films, luxury, prestige |

## Stacking Rules (Up to 3 Simultaneous)

You can combine up to 3 movements simultaneously. Examples:

### Combination Examples
- **Dolly In + Pan Right + Tilt Up** = complex three-axis tracking shot, following subject upward-right as camera advances
- **Handheld + Through Object + Pan** = documentary-style approach through foreground, discovering scene
- **Crane Up + 360 Orbit + Zoom Out** = establishing shot, revealing scale of environment
- **Dolly Out + Tilt Down + Zoom In** = paradoxical effect (move away, zoom in), creates tension
- **Arc Right + Tilt Up + Whip Pan** = flowing multi-axis motion
- **Static + Wiggle + Film Grain** = locked documentary with texture

### Technical Guidelines
1. **Dominant motion:** one primary movement (e.g., dolly in)
2. **Secondary motion:** one complementary movement (e.g., pan right to follow)
3. **Tertiary motion:** one accent or quality adjustment (e.g., handheld for texture)

Avoid stacking conflicting movements (e.g., both zoom in and dolly in can cancel each other). Intention matters.

## Camera Movement by Scene Type

### Establishing Shot
- **Crane Up + 360 Orbit** — reveals scale and environment
- **Dolly Out + Pan** — pulls back to show context
- **Super Dolly Out** — extreme pullback for grand reveal

### Character / Dialogue
- **Handheld + Subtle Dolly In** — intimate, approaching subject
- **Static + Subtle Wiggle** — safe, documentary feel
- **Steadicam + Arc** — smooth character introduction

### Action / Energy
- **Whip Pan + Crash Zoom** — fast, dynamic, impact
- **Dolly In + Tilt Up** — ascending drama, excitement
- **Handheld + Through Object** — chase feel, discovery

### Product Showcase
- **360 Orbit** — show all angles of object
- **Dolly In + Tilt** — reveal detail and scale
- **Steady Zoom In** — focus attention, clean

### Transitions
- **Whip Pan** — move between subjects fluidly
- **Fast Cut** — hard cut between compositions
- **Spiral / Arc** — flowing transition between scenes

### Emotional / Psychological
- **Dutch Angle** — unease, tension, disorientation
- **Handheld + Wiggle** — anxiety, chaos, uncertainty
- **Slow Crane + Tilt Down** — contemplation, sadness
- **Smooth Arc + Bloom** — dreamy, romantic, ethereal

## Performance Notes

### Generation Speed Impact
- **Static shots** — fastest
- **Single movement (dolly, pan, tilt)** — standard speed
- **2 combined movements** — standard speed
- **3 combined movements** — slower, more computation
- **Timelapse + complex motion** — slower

### Quality by Movement
- **Smooth movements** (dolly, crane, steadicam) — highest quality, most stable
- **Jerky movements** (whip pan, handheld) — intentional texture, feel
- **Rotations + orbits** — moderate compute, smooth output
- **Through-object** — requires scene understanding, occasional artifacts at obstacle boundary

## Creative Tips

1. **Lead with motivation** — justify every camera movement with character action or visual discovery
2. **Avoid aimless moves** — a camera movement should reveal something or follow someone
3. **Establish before moving** — let viewers see the shot, then move to reveal more
4. **Match movement to mood** — smooth for luxury, handheld for intimacy, fast for energy
5. **Move on the action** — time camera movement with subject action for invisible cuts
6. **Layer intentions** — combine pan + dolly to follow and reveal simultaneously
7. **Test short versions** — generate 5s clips with single movements before stacking 3
