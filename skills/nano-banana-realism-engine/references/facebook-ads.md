# Facebook and Meta Ad Guidance

When the request is for Facebook ads, Meta ads, paid social, or image tests, optimize for scroll-stop without looking like a designed ad.

## Principles

- UGC and real-life photography usually outperform polished studio looks when trust is the goal.
- The image should communicate the offer or the problem quickly even without text.
- Build for one audience moment per concept.

## High-performing visual angles

### Problem-aware
Show the frustration, mess, delay, clutter, inconvenience, tiredness, or pain point.

Examples:
- crowded hallway on moving day
- stressed family packing
- fogged bathroom mirror before skincare routine
- commuter looking stranded before a taxi arrives

### Aspiration-led
Show the after-state, ease, confidence, relief, comfort, or upgrade.

Examples:
- calm family settled into a new home
- warm kitchen featuring premium product use
- passenger stepping into a clean taxi at golden hour
- confident customer using the product naturally

### Product-in-use
The product should be handled or used in a plausible scene.

### Social proof or testimonial
Favor phone-camera realism, eye-level framing, and natural facial behavior.

## Split-test suggestions

For every ad concept, test one meaningful variable:
- human-first vs product-first
- indoor vs outdoor
- candid smartphone vs cleaner mirrorless
- before-state vs after-state
- solo subject vs couple/family
- premium tone vs everyday approachable tone

## Avoid

- fake stock-smile energy
- oversized product compared to hands
- too many props
- rendered text overlays unless explicitly requested
- generic “brand photoshoot” visuals that hide the offer
